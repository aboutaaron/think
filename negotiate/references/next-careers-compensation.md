# NEXT Careers — Compensation & Negotiation (Reference)

Source: NEXT Careers Circle community (no longer active). Preserved for reference.

## Know Your Market Value

Before first interview, know a compensation range reflecting:
- Experience, skills, credentials, and network you bring
- Location (yours and company HQ)
- Role and level
- Industry, size, and stage of startup

Know three numbers: lowest you'd be happy with, what you really want, what you'll ask for.

## Equity Types

- **Stock options** — right to buy stock at a fixed price (strike price)
- **RSUs** — stock awarded that vests over time with conditions
- **Tokens** — emerging; increasingly relevant in crypto/web3

Recommended: Carta Equity 101 series (10 short videos)

## Calculating Equity Value

Need to know:
- Strike price (cost to buy each unit)
- Current valuation
- Potential exit price
- Number of shares / total outstanding

Use 81cents Options Analysis Template or altLine Equity Investment Simulation for scenario modeling.

Pro tip: If offered options without a strike price, ask for the current/most recent strike price before negotiating.

For C-level / large equity packages: consult a startup attorney and/or CPA.

## Negotiation Strategy

### How to Negotiate
1. Never negotiate by email — context gets lost
2. After receiving verbal offer, ask for details in writing
3. Express excitement via email, ask for a quick call
4. Be prepared with a detailed script

### Beyond Base + Equity
- Start date
- Title
- Location / WFH flexibility
- WFH stipend
- L&D stipend
- Comp revisit at a specific date or triggering event
- Signing bonus

### What Matters to You (Prioritization)
- Potential upside in company value
- Brand name
- Title and responsibilities
- Culture
- Growth potential
- Benefits
- Location flexibility

### Managing Emotions
- Separate facts from emotional reactions
- Breathe if heart rate rises
- Don't over-empathize with hiring manager's logistics
- They want you — be confident
- Don't let ego get in the way
- Know what will make you happy going to work every day
