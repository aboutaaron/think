# NEXT Careers — Compensation & Negotiation (Reference)

Source: NEXT Careers Circle community (no longer active). Preserved for reference.

## Know Your Market Value

Before first interview, know a compensation range reflecting:
- Experience, skills, credentials, and network you bring
- Location (yours and company HQ)
- Role and level
- Industry, size, and stage of startup

Know three numbers: lowest you'd be happy with, what you really want, what you'll ask for.

## Equity Types

- **Stock options** — right to buy stock at a fixed price (strike price)
- **RSUs** — stock awarded that vests over time with conditions
- **Tokens** — emerging; increasingly relevant in crypto/web3

Recommended: Carta Equity 101 series (10 short videos)

## Calculating Equity Value

Need to know:
- Strike price (cost to buy each unit)
- Current valuation
- Potential exit price
- Number of shares / total outstanding

Use 81cents Options Analysis Template or altLine Equity Investment Simulation for scenario modeling.

Pro tip: If offered options without a strike price, ask for the current/most recent strike price before negotiating.

For C-level / large equity packages: consult a startup attorney and/or CPA.

## Negotiation Strategy

### How to Negotiate
1. Never negotiate by email — context gets lost
2. After receiving verbal offer, ask for details in writing
3. Express excitement via email, ask for a quick call
4. Be prepared with a detailed script

### Beyond Base + Equity
- Start date
- Title
- Location / WFH flexibility
- WFH stipend
- L&D stipend
- Comp revisit at a specific date or triggering event
- Signing bonus

### What Matters to You (Prioritization)
- Potential upside in company value
- Brand name
- Title and responsibilities
- Culture
- Growth potential
- Benefits
- Location flexibility

### Managing Emotions
- Separate facts from emotional reactions
- Breathe if heart rate rises
- Don't over-empathize with hiring manager's logistics
- They want you — be confident
- Don't let ego get in the way
- Know what will make you happy going to work every day

## Negotiation Scripts

### "What are your salary expectations?"
> "I'd like to learn more about the position and full compensation package since I don't have enough context to give you an exact number right now. What is the budget or range you have in mind for this position?"

If pressed for a number:
> "I know the average [job title] in [city] makes roughly [x] to [y] a year in salary so I think that is a good place to start."

### Asking for higher base or more equity
> "Thank you for the offer. I'm very excited about the position and know that I'd be the right fit for the team. I'm also excited about your offer, and know that I'll bring a lot of value to the table based on my experience that we discussed during the interviews.
>
> I'm wondering if we can explore a slightly higher starting salary of [amount higher than what you want to walk away with]. My market research showed that as the industry average for the area, and I'm confident that you'll be happy with how much I can contribute to the team and department."

### "We don't have room to negotiate"
> "I definitely understand budgeting issues, and I want to be as flexible as possible so that we can get to the outcome we both want of working together. Getting closer to that amount is important to get me onboard because it's [consistent with the market / a similar level to other opportunities I'm considering]. How can we collaborate to make this a win-win?"

Or pivot to benefits:
> "I understand that at this point, salary isn't negotiable. Would you consider including [this benefit] as part of the package?"
