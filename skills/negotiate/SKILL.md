# Negotiate

Prepare a compensation negotiation strategy. Don't let someone else assign your value.

## Step 1: Know Your Market Value

Before any negotiation, establish your range. Ask:

1. What's the role, level, and title?
2. What's the company stage and size? (Seed, Series A/B/C, public)
3. Where are you located? Where's the company HQ?
4. What's the lowest number you'd be happy with?
5. What do you *really* want?
6. What will you *ask for* (so you walk away with what you really want)?

### Benchmarking Resources

Suggest these for research:
- [H1B Salary Database](https://h1bdata.info/) — employer-reported, most reliable
- [Levels.fyi](https://levels.fyi/) — self-reported but widely used
- [Blind](https://www.teamblind.com/salaryComparison) — anonymous self-reported
- [Carta Equity 101](https://carta.com/equity/learn/) — 10 short videos on equity fundamentals

## Step 2: Evaluate the Full Package

Help the user see beyond base salary:

| Component | What to evaluate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Base salary | Market rate for role, level, location, stage |
| Equity | Type (options vs RSUs), strike price, vesting schedule, current valuation, potential exit value |
| Bonus | Signing bonus, performance bonus, retention bonus |
| Benefits | Health, 401k match, PTO, parental leave, L&D stipend, WFH stipend |
| Other | Title, start date, location flexibility, comp review timing |

### Equity Evaluation

If offered stock options, the user needs:
- Strike price (cost to buy each unit)
- Current valuation
- Number of shares and total outstanding (to calculate ownership %)
- Vesting schedule (typically 4 years, 1-year cliff)
- Potential exit scenarios

If the user doesn't have the strike price: **ask for it before negotiating anything else.**

For C-level or large equity packages: recommend consulting a startup attorney and/or CPA.

## Step 3: Build the Strategy

Ask: **"What matters most to you beyond money?"** Options to explore:

- Potential upside in company value
- Brand name / resume value
- Title and scope of responsibilities
- Culture and team
- Growth potential (personal and within company)
- Location flexibility
- Learning & development budget

Then prioritize: what will you push hard on, what will you concede, what's a dealbreaker?

## Step 4: Script the Conversation

**Rule: Never negotiate by email.** Context gets lost.

The flow:
1. Receive the offer (usually verbal)
2. Ask for details in writing: "Could you send that over in an email so I can review the full picture?"
3. Reply expressing excitement + ask for a quick call: "I'm really excited about this. Do you have a few minutes to run through some questions?"
4. On the call: use your prepared script

### Levers Beyond the Obvious

If salary is firm, negotiate:
- Start date
- Title
- Location / remote flexibility
- WFH stipend
- L&D budget
- Comp review at a specific date or triggering event (e.g., next funding round)
- Signing bonus

## Step 5: Manage the Moment

Coaching points for the actual conversation:
- Separate facts from emotions
- Breathe if your heart rate rises
- Don't over-empathize with the hiring manager's constraints — that's their problem to solve
- They want you. Be confident in that.
- Don't let ego override what will actually make you happy day-to-day
- Know your walk-away number before the call starts

## Ready-Made Scripts

When the user is preparing for the actual conversation, offer these scripts adapted to their situation:

- **"What are your salary expectations?"** → Deflect to learning more, ask for their range first
- **Asking for more** → Express excitement, reference market research, ask for a number above your target
- **"We can't negotiate"** → Pivot to win-win framing or alternative levers (benefits, title, review timing)

See `references/next-careers-compensation.md` for full script templates.

Also suggest: [81cents Script Builder](https://81c.typeform.com/to/EVb7AwFP) — generates a custom negotiation script from your offer details.

## Guidelines

- **Comp conversations should start early.** Be prepared for range questions in phone screens.
- **Never give a number first if you can avoid it.** Ask for their range instead.
- **The ask should be higher than what you want.** Build in room to "meet in the middle" at your actual target.
- **Get it in writing.** Verbal agreements mean nothing until they're in the offer letter.
- **Silence is a negotiation tool.** After stating your ask, stop talking.
