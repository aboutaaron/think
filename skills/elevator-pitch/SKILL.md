# Elevator Pitch

Build a personal brand statement and elevator pitch that positions you for the right opportunities.

## Step 1: Define Your Brand

Ask the user these questions one at a time:

1. What are your strengths?
2. What settings do you work best in? (Early stage, late stage, big company, as a builder, etc.)
3. What personality trait are you known for?
4. What results do you achieve?
5. What is your career moat — the thing that's hard to replicate?
6. Who do you want to pay attention to you?

## Step 2: Build the "I Help" Statement

From the answers, craft a concise positioning statement:

> "I help [target audience] do [result you achieve] by [your strengths and style]."

Example: "I help early stage companies hire the talent they need by creating scalable people ops systems."

Present 2-3 options and let the user pick or blend.

## Step 3: Build the Story

Structure the pitch using one of these frameworks based on the user's situation:

**Same role, different industry:**
1. What you do now + key skills + what you're looking for
2. Career highlights that transfer to the target industry
3. How you're building knowledge in the new space
4. Why you're excited + the value you'll bring

**Different role, same industry:**
1. Theme connecting your transferable skills + industry experience
2. Career highlights with transferable skills and results
3. How you're building toward the target role
4. Why you're excited + the value you'll bring

**More senior role, same track:**
1. What you do now + key skills + what you're looking for
2. Career highlights + management/leadership experience
3. Why you're ready for the next level
4. Why you're excited + the value you'll bring

## Step 4: Tailor by Length

Create three versions:

- **30-second** — Quick intro at a networking event
- **1-2 minute** — Coffee chat or friendly networking
- **2-2.5 minute** — "Tell me about yourself" in an interview

## Step 5: Audience-Tune

Before finalizing, ask: **"Who specifically will you be saying this to?"**

Then adjust the pitch to hit what matters to that listener:
- Founder → speed, resourcefulness, building from scratch
- Hiring manager → results, team fit, domain expertise  
- Peer → shared interests, collaboration potential
- Investor/board → strategic thinking, scale, market insight

## Guidelines

- **Know your listener before you speak.** The best pitch is tuned to what the other person cares about.
- **Lead with a theme, not a resume.** "I turn messy data into clear decisions" beats "I'm an engineering manager with 10 years of experience."
- **Show, don't tell.** One specific result beats three vague claims.
- **Practice out loud.** Written pitches sound different spoken. Record yourself and iterate.
- **It's a story, not a list.** Theme → characters (you) → plot (your arc) → style (your personality).
