## Defining Your Personal Brand

A "personal brand" can be described as:
- How others will describe you without saying your job title or company
- How you show your value to the world
- Your personality
- The conscious and intentional effort to create and influence public perception

### Self-Assessment Questions
- What are my strengths?
- What settings do I work best in? (Early stage startup, late stage startup, non-technical, as a co-founder…)
- What personality trait(s) am I known for?
- What results do I achieve?
- What is my career moat?
- Who do I want to pay attention to me?

### "I Help" Statement Format
"I help ___ [target audience] do ___ [result you achieve] by ___ [strengths and personality traits]."

## Elevator Pitch Frameworks

### Understanding Your Listener
Before pitching, understand what will resonate with your listener and what you want them to do with the information.

Example: Pre-series A founder likely cares about speed, unstructured environment comfort, building from scratch, and revenue.

### Attributes That Signal Startup Readiness
- Growth mindset
- Adaptability & resilience
- Agile in unstructured environments
- Resourceful
- Comfortable with experimentation
- High accountability

### Framework 1: Same Role, Different Industry
1. Start with what you do now, how you do it, what you're looking for
2. Highlights of career moat with transferable skills and results
3. How you're building knowledge in the target industry
4. Why you're excited + value you'll bring

### Framework 2: Different Role, Same Industry
1. Theme relating your transferable skills + industry experience
2. Highlights with transferable skills, results, and past role transitions
3. How you're building toward the target role
4. Why you're excited + value you'll bring

### Framework 3: More Senior, Similar Path
1. What you do now, how you do it, what's next
2. Highlights with management experience mapped to target
3. Why you're ready
4. Why you're excited + value you'll bring

## Pitch Length Guidelines
- Quick intro: 30 seconds to 1 minute
- Coffee chat / networking: 1 to 2 minutes
- "Tell me about yourself": no longer than 2.5 minutes

## Practice Method
1. Write out each version
2. Review what you wrote
3. Practice out loud
4. Record yourself
5. Iterate until comfortable and confident
