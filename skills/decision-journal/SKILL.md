# Decision Journal

Log decisions so you can learn from them later. This is the personal version of the decision infrastructure you argue every company should build.

## Logging a Decision

When the user makes a decision, capture:

### Decision Entry

```markdown
## [Date] — [One-line decision summary]

**What I decided:** [The specific choice made]

**Alternatives considered:** [What else was on the table and why it was rejected]

**What I expect to happen:** [Specific, observable outcome — not vague hopes]

**Confidence:** [High / Medium / Low] — and why

**What would change my mind:** [What evidence would tell me this was wrong]

**Review date:** [When to revisit — 2 weeks, 1 month, 1 quarter]

**Context I might forget:** [Emotional state, time pressure, information I had/didn't have, who influenced me]
```

## Reviewing a Decision

When the review date arrives (or the user asks to review past decisions):

1. Read the original entry — including the "context I might forget" section
2. Ask: **What actually happened?**
3. Compare expected vs actual outcome
4. Ask: **Was I right for the right reasons, right for the wrong reasons, or wrong?**
5. Extract the lesson — one sentence

### Review Entry (append to original)

```markdown
**Review [date]:**
- **What actually happened:** [...]
- **Verdict:** [Right/right reasons | Right/wrong reasons | Wrong]
- **Lesson:** [One sentence]
- **PHV connection:** [Did this update any House View? If so, which one]
```

## What's Worth Journaling

Not every choice needs a journal entry. Focus on:

- **Irreversible or expensive-to-reverse decisions** — hiring, role changes, strategy shifts, saying no to something
- **Decisions where you're uncertain** — the ones where you could argue either side
- **Decisions influenced by emotion** — excitement, fear, pressure, ego
- **Recurring decisions** — if you keep facing the same choice, the journal reveals your pattern

Skip: routine choices, obvious calls, decisions you can easily undo.

## Guidelines

- **"Context I might forget" is the most important field.** In 3 months you won't remember that you were exhausted, or that your boss was pressuring you, or that you only had half the data. Write it down now.
- **Confidence calibration is the point.** Over time, you'll learn whether your "high confidence" calls are actually right more often than your "low confidence" ones. If not, your confidence is miscalibrated — that's the most valuable thing the journal can teach you.
- **One sentence lessons only.** If the lesson takes a paragraph, you haven't distilled it yet.
- **Connect to House Views.** Decisions are where views meet reality. If a decision outcome surprises you, check whether a PHV needs updating.
